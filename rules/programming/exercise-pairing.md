# Exercise Pairing & Logistics

## Summary

Rules for how exercises are paired within a series, equipment logistics across gym locations, C-series design, and home workout scheduling. These exist to keep sessions flowing and avoid equipment conflicts on the floor.

## Rules

### Pairings Must Work at Both Gym Locations

- Any program pairing must be executable at both Bridge and Bligh Street without equipment conflict.
- Examples of pairings that do **NOT** work:
  - Barbell Squat + Leg Curl — works at Bligh but not Bridge.
  - Flat Press + Leg Curl Machine — could end up at opposite ends of the gym at Bligh.
  - Barbell Squat paired with Barbell Press or DB Press — too much equipment and setup in one spot.

### C-Series Must Be Self-Sufficient + Minimum Equipment

- If the session runs over time, members must be able to continue independently without key equipment or injury risk.
- Minimise machine and bench usage in C-series to prevent floor overflow.
- **Good C-series options:** Arm work, core exercises.
- **Avoid in C-series:** Hip thrusts, leg press, any exercise requiring a bench, any machine likely needed for the next class.

### Home Workouts

- Home workouts must be loaded on weekends.
- Exception: if the client prefers the home workout on the exact day they will do it, label it clearly so coaches know not to coach it (e.g. *"Home Workout / Day to Complete on Your Own"*).

### Additional Work To Do On Own

- Extra client work must be clearly labelled in the title.
- Should be simple, minimal setup, and easy for clients to do unsupervised.

### Rehab Integration

- Rehab work counts toward total set volume.
- Must be factored into overall workload.

## Site Exceptions

> No site-specific exceptions currently — but the pairing-logistics rules are specifically about Bridge and Bligh equipment layouts.

## Examples

**Example 1: Good pairing**

A-Series: DB Bench Press + Seated Cable Row — equipment is close together at both locations.

**Example 2: Bad pairing**

A-Series: Barbell Squat + Leg Curl Machine — at Bridge, these are in different areas and create setup delays.

**Example 3: C-Series done right**

C-Series: Tricep Pushdown + Dead Bug — no bench, no machine likely needed by incoming class.
